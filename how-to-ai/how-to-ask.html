How to ask AI for help

You don’t need special formulas. You need the same clarity you’d give a new hire on day one.

7 min read · For non-coders

Weak request: “Write a Facebook post.” Strong request: “Write a short Facebook post for a neighborhood bakery. Warm tone. Mention Saturday’s blueberry scones. End with a soft invite to stop by. Keep it under 80 words.”

The four pieces of a good ask

  1. Role or context — who you are, or who the audience is
  2. Goal — what “done” looks like
  3. Constraints — length, tone, must-include facts
  4. Example — a sentence you like, or one to avoid

Try this template

“I’m a [role]. I need [deliverable] for [audience]. Tone should be [tone]. Include [facts]. Don’t mention [off-limits]. Give me [number] options.”

Then refine

If the first answer is almost right, don’t start over. Say: “Shorter.” “More local.” “Less salesy.” “Keep the second option and make it friendlier.” Conversation beats perfection on try one.

One habit that changes everything

Always paste the facts that must be true — hours, prices, names, dates. AI invents details when you leave gaps. You stay the editor of reality.
